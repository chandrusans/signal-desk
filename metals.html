How range projection works

Read once, remember always

01Day range

Expected range = current price ± 0.5 × ATR₁₄. That's one standard-deviation-ish band for a normal trading day. About 68% of days stay within it; the other 32% break out with a catalyst.

02Week range

Expected weekly range = current price ± ATR₁₄ × √5. Variance scales with the square root of time — five trading days is roughly 2.24× the single-day band, not 5×.

03Direction bias

Trend is set by the MA20 slope and price location vs MA20/50/200. Slope up + price above the stack = long bias. Slope down + price below = short bias. Flat = range-bound.

04Break-of-range signals

A close outside the projected day range with expanding volume is a breakout signal — the range failed and a new trend leg is likely starting. Manage stops around the recent swing.

Metals drivers · what pushes the price

Live macro context

DXYUS Dollar Index

Dollar down = metals up (they're priced in dollars). Gold especially inverse-correlated to DXY over multi-week windows. Watch 103 as a psychological pivot.

10YUS Treasury yield

Rising real yields = headwind for gold (no coupon to compete with). Falling yields, especially on soft inflation data = tailwind.

VIXRisk regime

VIX above 20 typically supports gold (safe-haven bid). Silver is more industrial — hurt more by growth fears than helped by risk-off.

RATIOGold-Silver ratio

XAU / XAG. Above 85 = silver historically cheap vs gold. Below 60 = silver expensive. Reversion trades one against the other.

    Not investment advice

    Range projections are statistical bands, not forecasts. A 1σ band contains ~68% of outcomes — the other 32% break it, and those are exactly the days that matter. Metals trade around Fed / CPI / geopolitical catalysts that no ATR model can predict. Treat the projected range as a base-case scaffold, not a promise.
